This research project examines the shift in consumer trust from
traditional **celebrity endorsements** to **social media influencer
marketing**, focusing on the role of **parasocial bonds** in shaping
credibility and purchase intention.

The study empirically compares influencer and celebrity endorsements
under matched conditions to identify what drives trust in modern digital
environments.

## ðŸ§ª Methodology

-   **Design:** Cross-sectional survey\
-   **Platform:** Qualtrics\
-   **Sample Size:** 102 valid respondents\
-   **Generations Covered:** Gen Z, Millennials, Gen X\
-   **Analysis Tool:** SPSS

-   Transparency / Disclosure Clarity\

-   Parasocial Bond (PSB)\

### Key Statistical Highlights:

-   Parasocial Bond was the strongest predictor of credibility (Î² =
    .595, p \< .001).
-   Parasocial Bond was also the strongest predictor of purchase

The survey data confirms that **relational closeness, not just message
transparency, drives modern endorsement trust**.

## ðŸ“ˆ Key Findings

1.  Influencer credibility is primarily driven by parasocial bonding.
2.  Influencers outperform celebrities even when message content is
    controlled.

    operates through relational connection.
